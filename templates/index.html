Understanding Brain Tumors

Brain tumors are abnormal growths of cells within the brain or skull. They can be benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous). Early detection is crucial for successful treatment and improved patient outcomes.

Our AI-powered system analyzes MRI scans to assist healthcare professionals in identifying potential brain tumors with high accuracy, providing valuable insights for timely medical intervention.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the AI detection?

Our AI system has been trained on thousands of MRI scans and achieves over 95% accuracy in identifying brain tumors. However, it should always be used in conjunction with professional medical diagnosis.

What image formats are supported?

The system supports common image formats including JPEG, PNG, and DICOM files. For best results, use high-resolution MRI scans in axial, coronal, or sagittal views.

Is my data secure?

Yes, all uploaded images are processed securely and are not stored on our servers. Your medical data privacy is our top priority.

Can this replace a doctor's diagnosis?

No, this tool is designed to assist healthcare professionals, not replace them. Always consult with a qualified medical professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

How long does analysis take?

The AI analysis typically takes less than 30 seconds to complete, providing rapid insights for healthcare decision-making.

What types of brain tumors can be detected?

The system can identify various types of brain tumors including gliomas, meningiomas, and pituitary tumors, distinguishing between benign and malignant growths.
